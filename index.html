Background

During the mid to late 2100’s the climate of earth deteriorated to a point where life on earth was nearly untenable. This period is known popularly as The Great Diaspora. It is also referred to in history as the Anthropocene Climax. During the twenty-second and twenty-third centuries, large scale human settlement was impossible on almost every land mass. Coastal cities were abandoned to rising tides. The desalination of the oceans due to the melting of polar ice disrupted or destroyed all of the major ocean currents, starving the ocean of nutrients. Dry summers scorched fields and farmlands in great fires. Hurricanes and Typhoons with the strength to blow down steel towers pushed ever farther inland.

Those with the means to leave earth fled to colonies on Mars and the Moons of Jupiter. From there, colonists spread outward through the solar system. To the asteroid belt, where mining collectives built colonies deep within large asteroids. Humans spread to the moon of Saturn, Titan, and further, even reaching the Sol system’s closest neighbors; Bernard’s Star, the Sirius binary system, and the trinary stars of the Centauri System, around the smallest of which they met the Avari, a sapient species that built towers not unlike those built on earth before the Great Calamity of the twenty-second century.

Most of those who were left on earth perished from starvation or natural disaster. Some clung to life in small communities or in the ruins of cities. Some nations or fragments of former nations kept lit the fire of civilization through great feats of ingenuity; such as the Republic of Singapore, and Jakarta Baru.
